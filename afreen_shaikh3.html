Hazrat Adam (A.S.)

The first human being and the first Prophet of Allah in Islamic belief

Introduction

Hazrat Adam (A.S.), known as Adam in Abrahamic religions, is regarded as the first human being and the first Prophet of Allah in Islamic belief. He is honored as the father of mankind and a key figure in the creation of humanity. His life is described in the Holy Qur'an, as well as in Hadith and earlier scriptures such as the Torah and Bible.

According to Islamic teachings, Allah created Adam (A.S.) from clay. Allah shaped him perfectly and breathed into him His spirit, granting him life and knowledge. Adam was given wisdom, intellect, and the ability to distinguish between right and wrong. He was taught the names of all things by Allah, which showed his superiority over the angels in terms of knowledge.

Creation and Prostration of Angels

Allah commanded the angels to prostrate before Adam as a sign of respect (not worship). All angels obeyed except Iblis (Satan), who refused out of pride and arrogance. This marked the beginning of enmity between Satan and mankind.

"And We said, 'O Adam, dwell, you and your wife, in Paradise and eat therefrom in [ease and] abundance from wherever you will. But do not approach this tree, lest you be among the wrongdoers.'" (Quran 2:35)

Life in Paradise

Adam and his wife Hawwa (Eve) were placed in Paradise, where they enjoyed all its blessings. They were given permission to eat from all trees except one, which Allah forbade them from approaching. Satan deceived them into eating from the forbidden tree, leading to their descent to Earth.

Life on Earth and Responsibilities

Adam (A.S.) became the first Prophet on Earth. Allah guided him and gave him laws to follow and teach his children. He taught people about Tawheed (belief in one God), morality, and righteous living. Adam was a farmer and learned basic skills for survival.

Adam and Hawwa had many children, including sons and daughters. The most well-known among them were Habil (Abel) and Qabil (Cain). Family of Adam (A.S.)

Hazrat Adam (A.S.) and Hawwa are considered the ancestors of all humanity. According to Islamic sources, they had many offspring who spread across the Earth. Every generation began from their lineage, making them the universal parents of mankind.

Death of Hazrat Adam (A.S.)

Adam (A.S.) lived for approximately 930 years (according to some traditions). When his time came, the Angel of Death took his soul. He was buried on Earth, and his children mourned his passing. Some Islamic narrations mention that he was buried in a sacred place, but the exact location is unknown.
